Organic Loaders

A catalogue of loading indicators derived directly from the F×D tuples of the ideasthesia notation. Each loader is an honest expression of one Form and one Dynamics — not a decoration, not a metaphor.

A. Shirs & Claude (Anthropic) — April 2026

The motivation is plain: most loading indicators are ornaments. They spin because spinning is what loaders do. The F×D schema gives a different starting point — it asks what kind of waiting is actually happening. A query that will return shortly is not the same shape of time as a long computation that may collapse. A process that returns to its origin (polling) is not the same shape as one that reaches forward (streaming).

Each cell below pairs a Form with a Dynamics and lets that tuple dictate the motion. The geometry is doing the work. The captions are notation, not marketing.

On the translation from notation to motion

The mapping is direct, not imaginative. Form answers what the trajectory is — a point, a line, a closed curve, a confluence, a contraction. Dynamics answers what that trajectory does over time — holds, returns, drifts, or ends. Put a Form with a Dynamics and the motion is almost forced on you. The designer's remaining choices are tempo, scale, and accent.

Two small commitments make this legible as a system rather than a set of poses. First, the terminal Dynamics always draws in orange; the non-terminal Dynamics always draws in the chosen accent. Second, all nine loaders share the same stroke weight and the same roughly 60-pixel working radius. Nothing here is size or color; the variable is qualitative.

Not in scope. Progress-bearing loaders (determinate percentages) are a different object and are not modelled here — they would require a Packing-Dim extension for the "how much has accumulated" dimension. Skeleton loaders are also omitted; they describe layout, not dynamics.
